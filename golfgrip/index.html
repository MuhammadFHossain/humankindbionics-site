The problem

Sore hands cannot hold the club.

Every long iron hurts the thumbs. By the turn, your hands ache and your grip loosens, so the club twists and the shots spray. Some days the arthritis is so bad you cannot hold a club at all, so you skip the round and your friends play without you.

Bigger grips help a little. Taping the thumbs helps a little. Neither puts the strength back in the hand.

GolfGrip holds part of the grip for you, so your hands stay steady and your joints take less.

How it works

Three steps.

1

Wear it like a golf glove

A leather glove, worn the way you already wear one. It carries part of the grip through the back of the hand.

2

Set the grip

A small dial at the wrist sets how much the glove holds. No battery. Set it once before the round.

3

Swing

The glove holds part of the grip, so you can grip lightly and the club stays put. Your thumbs and knuckles take far less.

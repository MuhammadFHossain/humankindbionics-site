Straight talk

Straps work, until they do not.

You re-tie them every set. They cut into your wrists. They do nothing on the days your hands are wrecked from a hurt forearm or a healing finger. And past forty, your grip fades while your legs and back are still strong.

This is not a competition trick, and we will not pretend it is. If you want a raw, no-help pull, this is not for that. It is for training volume when your grip is the bottleneck, coming back from an injury, and lifting for the long haul.

It shares the grip along your fingers, goes on in seconds, and dials up or down by the set.

How it works

Three steps.

1

Glove up

A low-profile glove. Strong cables run along your fingers. No bulk between you and the bar.

2

Set the load

A dial at the wrist sets how much the glove holds. No battery. Crank it for a heavy set, back it off for warmups.

3

Pull

The glove holds part of the grip, so the bar does not roll out. No re-tying between sets, no cutting into your wrists.

Who it is for

Older lifters, grip recovery, and the strap-averse.

The problem

Gripping the paddle too hard wears you out.

You would play every day if your body let you. But gripping the paddle hard hurts your elbow, your hand is sore by the second game, and some mornings the arthritis is too much. So you sit out games you would rather play.

A cushioned grip and a copper sleeve take the edge off. They do not hold the paddle for you.

PickleGrip does. It holds part of the grip, so you can play looser and longer, and your hand takes less.

How it works

Three steps.

1

Put it on

A light glove that carries part of the grip for you, so holding the paddle takes less out of your hand.

2

Set the grip

A dial at the wrist sets how much the glove holds. No battery, no charging. Set it before you play.

3

Play

The glove keeps the paddle steady, so you grip lightly. Less strain on the elbow and hand, more games before you tap out.
